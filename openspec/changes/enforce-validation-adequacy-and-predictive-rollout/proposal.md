## Why

PhysicsGuard's current validation-depth receipt proves exact identities, declared scope, and pointwise execution, but it can still accept a tiny or badly distributed sample as a broad time or dataset claim. Predictive claims also need a target-owned future-holdout rollout contract so pointwise residual agreement cannot be mistaken for trajectory prediction.

## What Changes

- Add target-owned adequacy evidence derived from the raw manifest, hierarchy, model binding, and selected observed series: available, eligible, selected, and validated counts/ratios plus per-signal and signal-by-time coverage.
- Require distinct timestamps, positive temporal span, start/middle/end strata, declared event coverage, duplicate detection, and bounded maximum gaps for broad temporal claims.
- Add explicit `full`, `stratified`, `event_aware`, `adaptive`, and `project_declared` sampling modes with threshold provenance and pass/partial/block adequacy status.
- Require non-empty broad-scope variable roles, critical-signal and critical-parameter coverage, subsystem/family quotas, bounded exclusions, and non-template exclusion rationales.
- Bind every sample to a predeclared selection-policy id/rationale and derive temporal phases from the raw row universe, so selected points cannot renormalize themselves into apparent full-window coverage.
- Bind every generated guard-model candidate to the exact frozen prevented-failure contract through a target-owned candidate artifact and ordered hash-linked authoring events. Missing, stale/mismatched, or candidate-before-purpose artifacts fail closed.
- Distinguish target-native semantic detection from obligation-admission proof. A generic missing-obligation result can prove only that an unproved candidate is rejected; it cannot be relabeled as detection of a physical, mapping, topology, or evidence defect.
- Give every time-varying parameter its own target-owned denominator, count/ratio/distinct-time/span/gap floors, universal early/middle/late and project stages; keep static parameters on a binding-only gate and block one shallow parameter even when siblings are deep.
- Prefer full vectorized row validation when the aligned sequence is available; representative sampling remains bounded and additionally requires per-parameter residual, observed-direction, and physical-envelope evidence.
- Separate `pointwise` from `stateful_dynamic` model semantics. Pointwise evidence can never authorize prediction; predictive claims require a disjoint future-holdout rollout with initial state, step, accumulated error, lag/phase, stability, and drift evidence.
- Make project closure compare the requested claim with the receipt's covered scope, adequacy, and model semantics; snapshots cannot satisfy validation-ready closure and only a passing trajectory receipt can satisfy predictive closure.
- Extend skills, templates, documentation, FlowGuard models, and SkillGuard V2
  static-positive/time-varying-positive/shallow content-addressed calibration;
  ordinary supervision executes the plan from exact target inputs without
  creating a parallel SkillGuard physics evaluator.
- Require scheduled-production identity to come from exactly one target-owned identity sidecar in the declared input set, and bundle a content-manifested complete PhysicsGuard runtime in the dataset-validation installation projection.
- Project one fixed `native-integrated` identity from every maintained PhysicsGuard target into SkillGuard: the exact target owner/default route, required route/check binding wire, and non-optional `skillguard.depth_profile.v2` inventory for every purpose, candidate, known-good, and known-bad check are mandatory before the sole enforced closure; the compiler must preserve that profile, while parallel or SkillGuard-owned runtime routes remain forbidden.
- **BREAKING** for new broad validation plans: previously optional or self-declared coverage details become required for non-snapshot pass claims. Snapshot and explicitly bounded legacy work remain available with downgraded claims.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

- `predictive-rollout-validation`: Target-owned future-holdout trajectory validation and predictive claim gating for stateful dynamic models.

### Modified Capabilities

- `simulation-validation-depth`: Add quantitative signal/time/model coverage universes, sampling adequacy, broad-scope role and exclusion gates, and claim-scope-compatible closure.

## Impact

- Schemas and runtime logic under `src/physicsguard/schema/` and `src/physicsguard/core/`.
- Model-dataset validation reports, native receipts, project closure, templates, CLI-visible JSON, examples, and documentation.
- PhysicsGuard skill prompts for dataset validation, test-file contracts, signal mapping, evidence registry, debugging, and audit closure.
- Existing FlowGuard validation-depth, lifecycle, model-test alignment, and TestMesh evidence.
- Focused regression fixtures for shallow temporal/signal coverage and predictive overclaim.
